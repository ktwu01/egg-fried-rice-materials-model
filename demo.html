The four-part model

1. Egg coagulation (Avrami nucleation–growth, not first-order):
\[ \phi(t) = 1 - \exp\!\big[-(k(T)\,t)^n\big], \qquad k(T) = A\,e^{-E_a/RT} \]

2. Per-grain coverage: collision (builds θ) vs. shear fragmentation (tears θ):
\[ \frac{d\theta}{dt} = \beta_0 I \big(\theta_{\max}-\theta\big)\phi(t) \;-\; \gamma_0 I^2 \theta \] Collision rate grows linearly with stirring \(I\); shear-induced debonding grows quadratically — so unlike the original model's monotonic \(e^{-I/I_0}\), coverage is non-monotonic in \(I\): too little stirring never spreads the egg, too much shreds it back off. That trade-off is what the I* sweep on the left is finding numerically.

3. Stoichiometric ceiling — kinetics can't beat a lack of egg:
\[ \theta_{\max}(r) = \min\!\left(1,\ \frac{r}{r_c}\right) \] where \(r\) is the egg:rice mass ratio and \(r_c\) the ratio needed to give every grain a full monolayer.

4. Percolation criterion for "no isolated grains, no isolated egg":
\[ \text{bonded} \iff \langle\theta\rangle \ge \theta_c \ \text{ and } \ \mathrm{Var}(\theta) \le \mathrm{Var}_c \] A high mean with high variance just means some grains are bare and some are drowned in egg — both are failure modes the original model couldn't distinguish.
